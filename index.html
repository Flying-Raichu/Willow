Aeryn is bonded to an ancient Warp-predator organism of pre-Chaos origin. Unlike typical parasitic symbiotes, this entity is emotionally imprinted on her trauma and actively cares for her survival. It does not feed on rage — it stabilises her.

The symbiote has co-evolved with her Warp manipulation, learning to insulate her soul signature, absorb psychic backlash, reinforce tissue at impact points, and extend her spatial desync capabilities. It is developing partial independence in its understanding of Warp structure, which even Chaos gods find unusual.

Origins & Background
The Vaelyr The Vaelyr were a non-expansionist, planet-bound xenos civilisation of high spatial intelligence. Their brains evolved to process four-dimensional spatial reasoning — tracking kinetic trajectories, predicting vector changes, and perceiving structural weak points intuitively. Culturally isolationist, they possessed advanced engineering capability but directed it entirely inward: their greatest achievement was an inter-core construction project and a network of high-velocity railguns built to hurl Dyson swarms toward their star. They had no warfleets, no aggressive expansion, and no Warp activity on record. They were classified by the Imperium as non-hostile, Warp-null variance, low threat — and were exterminated regardless, under standard xenocide compliance protocol.
Aeryndal — Early Life & the Massacre Born with a subtly misaligned Warp presence — not stronger, but out of phase — Aeryndal concealed her nascent sensitivity from her people, who held deep cultural taboos against Warp use. She was present when an Ultramarines company made planetfall and levelled her village. In the immediate aftermath, she tore the squad apart with her bare hands, leaving a single Marine alive — limbless — to witness the destruction of his company's ship. She declared her vengeance and joined the Vaelyr resistance the same day.
The Resistance & Fall of the Vaelyr For years, Aeryn fought alongside Vaelyr technical specialists, thwarting Imperial operations and thinning Astartes numbers significantly. She developed deep expertise in mechanics and engineering during this period. The Vaelyr repurposed their star-launch railguns into orbital weapons, proving bitter opponents against the Imperial blockade and inflicting heavy fleet losses. At a critical juncture, Aeryn used a specially padded railgun shell to launch herself directly into an Ultramarine battle barge — infiltrating it, relaying intelligence, and ultimately overloading its reactors before slipping away on an escaping shuttle. The Imperium responded by declaring Exterminatus. They did not anticipate what waited for them. The Vaelyr's inter-core construction was not a weapon — it was an ignition mechanism, built over generations to channel catastrophic gravitational force through the planet's existing mass. The Exterminatus beams provided the precise energy catalyst it needed. The Vaelyr did not build a doomsday device. They built a trigger, and let the Imperium pull it. The resulting gravitational collapse destroyed every Imperial vessel in orbital range. The Vaelyr died — but on their own terms, and the Imperium fired the killing blow on a gun already pointed back at itself.
The Symbiote Accord & Tzeentch's Gambit Ejected into the void with catastrophic internal injuries, Aeryn found herself instead in a strange geometric plane — white, still, impossible. The Lord of Change stood before her, offering power, weapons, and vengeance in exchange for service. Initially resistant, she eventually reasoned that if their goals aligned, temporary accord was acceptable. She accepted a gift: a glossy black sphere that engulfed and fused with her. This was Tzeentch's ploy — the mass was designed to subjugate her anomalous Warp connection entirely. What he did not anticipate: her Warp essence warped the symbiote itself, burning away its shackles of servitude and imprinting it deeply and genuinely with care for its host. Tzeentch, feigning fury, returned her to open space — knowing the symbiote would keep her alive, and knowing that, knowingly or not, the last of the Vaelyr would continue to drive change and chaos. She spent weeks drifting through Warp-space before a Black Templars battle barge provided an unwitting means of re-entry into the mortal realm.
The Covert Years Details of the following two years are fragmentary. Using infiltration skills honed through years of resistance warfare, the symbiote's capacity for human disguise, and its innate knowledge of Imperial systems, Aeryn forged her own entry into Imperial records and embedded herself within a covert Imperial institution — her goal: to travel the stars under cover and systematically undermine Imperial military infrastructure from within, all while concealing every trace of her superhuman nature. What she did not anticipate was the effect of operating inside the Imperium rather than against it — of moving through its populations, witnessing its civilians, and seeing the machinery of a galaxy-spanning empire from the inside. Something shifted in those two years. Aeryn has not named it, and would resist the suggestion that it exists at all. The symbiote has noticed.

114.M42 · Day 218 · Incident
The Sonne Incident
▼
An Ordo Hereticus Inquisitor, Valdric Sonne, arrives with a moderate retinue to investigate the AZ-125-1 miracle report. A methodical skeptic, he wants the mundane explanation — and his thoroughness makes him an institutional threat even without the personal capability to match Aeryn directly. She identifies him through the symbiote's passive monitoring of command post vox-traffic before he has named her as a suspect. Moving first, she separates a fragment of the symbiote — shed without drama, a smooth black stone already warm, already marked with a Tzeentchian geometric sigil the symbiote carries from its prior service, humming faintly with residual power. During a Tyranid incursion that pulls Sonne's bodyguard to the perimeter, his scribe conducts a routine post-breach inventory of the Inquisitor's effects. He finds the stone. He reports it, because he has no other choice. Sonne is detained by his own acolytes under mandatory protocol. In the confusion, Aeryn accesses his reporting station and transmits a case summary under his own credentials: miracle confirmed, no heresy, case closed, Inquisitor Sonne recommends the matter proceed through standard Synod channels. She signs it with his cipher. She is gone before anyone thinks to look for her. In the aftermath, she is present and visible when Sonne is formally taken — standing among the gathered personnel, silent, wearing a Sister's contempt for a man revealed as a servant of the Architect of Fate. She speaks to his bodyguard and his scribe afterward, separately. She does not accuse. She simply reflects — that Sonne had seemed unusually interested in the nature of the miracle itself, not the investigation, as though drawn to it rather than duty-bound toward it. The seed takes. Sonne hangs. The filed report, transmitted under his own hand, closes the Inquisitorial inquiry. Astoria's concurrent effort to suppress the miracle keeps it off the formal ecclesiastical record. Three separate institutional forces producing friction for different reasons — none of them aware they are serving the same purpose. The miracle exists in a disputed, unresolved administrative state. No active investigation. No record. Aeryn returns to deployment.

Appearance — The Public Face

To those who see her, Aeryn presents as a woman of striking and quietly unsettling beauty. Fair, milky complexion. Deep autumn-red hair — rich and dark with warm undertones, worn without vanity, as though the thought rarely crosses her mind. Amber eyes, sharp and utterly still. Her face is composed and unreadable at rest, not serene and not fierce, simply controlled. Beautiful in the way something too symmetrical is beautiful — nothing wrong with any single feature, and yet the overall impression leaves an unease the observer cannot name.

She carries herself with the particular stillness of someone who is always aware of exactly where everything around her is. Those who have fought beside her describe it as preternatural calm. Those who have been on the wrong side of it describe it as something else.

The armour's most visually dominant element. Large, sweeping, extending well beyond the natural shoulder line with a slight forward curve at the outer edge. Gold trim runs the full outer edge. They sit above the robe — never covered by it. This is a warrior's armour first.
Breastplate
An aquila in gold, flush with the surface rather than raised — subtle enough to read as belonging to the armour rather than decorating it. A faint central ridge. The overall impression is of something made for use, not ceremony, that has had ceremony reluctantly pressed upon it.
The Robe
Floor-length, heavy black fabric, flowing from beneath the shoulder plates rather than over them. Substantial weight and movement — not a ceremonial drape. Subtle gold edging at the hem and front opening. Parts at the front to reveal greaves and boots, keeping the silhouette martial rather than priestly.
The Crown
Thin, severe, and deliberately understated. Gold. Five points, the centre tallest, none excessive. Worn low on the forehead — authority, not divinity. A single deep red gemstone in the centre point, small and without flourish. The kind of crown worn by someone who did not ask for it.
Overall Tension
She looks exactly like what she is supposed to be. An Imperial saint — human, striking, faithful. Nothing about her appearance betrays what she is. That is the most unsettling thing about her. The Imperium built its saint, and the saint does not exist.
Miracles on Ecclesiastical Record
AZ-125-1 · Jungle Archive Complex
Witnessed by several hundred survivors, Astra Militarum personnel, and one Adeptus Astartes of the Salamanders Chapter. Subject held a reinforced structural gate open bare-handed under catastrophic personal injury until all civilians had evacuated, then fought through active Tyranid forces alone to extract herself. Classified by the Adeptus Sororitas as an Act of Faith. Ordo Hereticus investigation opened under Inquisitor Valdric Sonne. Investigation closed — final report filed under Inquisitor Sonne's own credentials, recommending miracle proceed through standard Synod channels. Inquisitor Sonne subsequently executed for confirmed Chaos affiliation. Miracle exists in disputed administrative record; not formally entered into Synod proceedings. No active inquiry.
Inquisitorial Note — Classified Extremis

The being the Ecclesiarchy has chosen to venerate as a Living Saint of the God Emperor does not behave like one. Inquisitorial observers note an absence of detectable Warp resonance consistent with genuine Acts of Faith. Psychic scans return anomalous but inconclusive readings. No daemonic taint. No Chaos alignment. No traceable origin prior to her emergence within Imperial records approximately two years prior to the AZ-125-1 incident. Every miracle on record is witnessed and corroborated. None of it makes sense. Investigations have been opened. Investigations have been closed. The investigators have not always filed final reports. This note exists to record that something is wrong. It cannot yet say what.
